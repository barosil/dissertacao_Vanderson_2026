# Referências Bibliográficas

## Radioastronomia Geral

1. **Wilson, T. L., Rohlfs, K., & Hüttemeister, S.** (2013). *Tools of Radio Astronomy*. Springer.

2. **Burke, B. F., & Graham-Smith, F.** (2009). *An Introduction to Radio Astronomy*. Cambridge University Press.

3. **Condon, J. J., & Ransom, S. M.** (2016). *Essential Radio Astronomy*. Princeton University Press.

## Single Dish Observations

4. **Mangum, J. G., & Wootten, A.** (1993). "Single dish calibration techniques at millimeter wavelengths". *PASP*, 105, 117-122.

5. **Wrobel, J. M., & Walker, R. C.** (1999). "Sensitivity". In *Synthesis Imaging in Radio Astronomy II* (Vol. 180, p. 171).

## Time Ordered Data e Mapmaking

6. **Natoli, P., et al.** (2001). "Iterative map-making with two-level preconditioning for polarized cosmic microwave background data sets". *A&A*, 372, 346-356.

7. **Kurki-Suonio, H., et al.** (2009). "Destriping CMB temperature and polarization maps". *A&A*, 506, 1511-1539.

8. **Poletti, D., et al.** (2019). "Making maps of Cosmic Microwave Background polarization with different scanning strategies". *A&A*, 600, A60.

## Projeto BINGO

9. **Battye, R. A., et al.** (2013). "BINGO: A single dish approach to 21cm intensity mapping". *arXiv:1209.1041*.

10. **Wuensche, C. A., et al.** (2020). "BINGO Collaboration updates". *J. Cosmology Astropart. Phys.*, 2020(05), 056.

## Intensity Mapping e BAO

11. **Bull, P., et al.** (2015). "Late-time cosmology with 21 cm intensity mapping experiments". *ApJ*, 803, 21.

12. **Santos, M. G., et al.** (2015). "Cosmology from a SKA HI intensity mapping survey". *Advancing Astrophysics with the Square Kilometre Array*, 19.

## Foregrounds

13. **Tegmark, M., & Efstathiou, G.** (1996). "A method for subtracting foregrounds from multifrequency CMB sky maps". *MNRAS*, 281, 1297-1314.

14. **Jelić, V., et al.** (2008). "Foreground simulations for the LOFAR-epoch of reionization experiment". *MNRAS*, 389, 1319-1335.

## HEALPix

15. **Górski, K. M., et al.** (2005). "HEALPix: A Framework for High-Resolution Discretization and Fast Analysis of Data Distributed on the Sphere". *ApJ*, 622, 759-771.

## Simulações

16. **Zonca, A., et al.** (2019). "healpy: equal area pixelization and spherical harmonics transforms for data on the sphere in Python". *JOSS*, 4(35), 1298.

17. **Thorne, B., et al.** (2017). "The Python Sky Model: software for simulating the Galactic microwave sky". *MNRAS*, 469, 2821-2833.

## Domain Driven Design

18. **Evans, E.** (2003). *Domain-Driven Design: Tackling Complexity in the Heart of Software*. Addison-Wesley.

19. **Vernon, V.** (2013). *Implementing Domain-Driven Design*. Addison-Wesley.

## Software Engineering para Ciência

20. **Wilson, G., et al.** (2014). "Best Practices for Scientific Computing". *PLoS Biology*, 12(1), e1001745.

21. **Wilson, G., et al.** (2017). "Good enough practices in scientific computing". *PLoS Computational Biology*, 13(6), e1005510.

## Python Scientific Stack

22. **Harris, C. R., et al.** (2020). "Array programming with NumPy". *Nature*, 585, 357-362.

23. **Virtanen, P., et al.** (2020). "SciPy 1.0: fundamental algorithms for scientific computing in Python". *Nature Methods*, 17, 261-272.

24. **Astropy Collaboration** (2018). "The Astropy Project: Building an Open-science Project and Status of the v2.0 Core Package". *AJ*, 156, 123.

## Calibration and Systematic Effects

25. **Perley, R. A., & Butler, B. J.** (2013). "An Accurate Flux Density Scale from 1 to 50 GHz". *ApJS*, 204, 19.

26. **Haslam, C. G. T., et al.** (1982). "A 408 MHz all-sky continuum survey. II - The atlas of contour maps". *A&AS*, 47, 1.

## Noise Analysis

27. **Wehus, I. K., et al.** (2017). "Bayesian analysis of sparse low-frequency radio surveys". *A&A*, 597, A131.

28. **Pardo, J. R., et al.** (2001). "Atmospheric transmission at microwaves (ATM): an improved model for millimeter/submillimeter applications". *IEEE Trans. Antennas Propag.*, 49, 1683-1694.

## Cosmologia

29. **Planck Collaboration** (2020). "Planck 2018 results. VI. Cosmological parameters". *A&A*, 641, A6.

30. **Eisenstein, D. J., et al.** (2005). "Detection of the Baryon Acoustic Peak in the Large-Scale Correlation Function of SDSS Luminous Red Galaxies". *ApJ*, 633, 560-574.
