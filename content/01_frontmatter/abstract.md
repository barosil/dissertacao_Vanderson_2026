# Abstract

**Keywords:** Radio Astronomy, Single Dish, Time Ordered Data, Mapmaking, Simulation, Pipeline

---

This dissertation presents the development of a sky simulation pipeline for Single Dish Radio Astronomy, focusing on Time Ordered Data (TOD) and mapmaking techniques. The work addresses the modeling of radio astronomical observations, considering instrumental, astrophysical, and computational aspects.

The developed pipeline enables the simulation of observations from the BINGO (Baryon Acoustic Oscillations from Integrated Neutral Gas Observations) radio telescope, including the generation of synthetic data, Time Ordered Data processing, and sky map reconstruction. The system was designed using Object-Oriented Programming principles and Domain Driven Design, ensuring extensibility and maintainability.

The results demonstrate the pipeline's capability to reproduce expected observational characteristics, including radiation patterns, instrumental noise, and systematic effects. Validation was performed through comparisons with theoretical models and independent simulations.

This work contributes to the development of simulation tools in radio astronomy, facilitating observation planning, processing algorithm testing, and scientific feasibility analysis of observational projects.
